A HELPING HAND
by Serenity "SwearyPrincess" Westfield

Gav slowed his hopping stride and sank onto his crutches, chest heaving. His dark hair was close-cropped, and he bore the puffy red complexion of those who couldn't afford even the cheapest circulation g-mods; he seemed to be almost fading away by the moment.

"Sara, how is some trash prankster going to help me?"

"He's not trash. Look — all the lights are on, the lift works, all the graffiti down here is current. Not a single tag from an old gang in sight."

They stopped outside a nondescript door. Just as Gav raised his hand to knock, it swung open and an empty wheelchair rolled out.

A voice called from somewhere inside. "Take a seat before you collapse. It'll bring you in."

---

Salonga wasn't what Gav expected. The person who asked to be called "Tāo" was a far cry from the kind of person the newsies warned about. Sitting in a battered old workstation chair, wearing a pastel bodysuit and warm smile. Dozens of servo mechanisms sat everywhere in various states of repair. Cables snaked around the furniture like vines.

"You're the one they said could help me get back on the work registry," Gav said.

Tāo looked at him for a long moment, then at the PAD in their lap. "The Weyland subsidiary that laid you off. You were on H-nine eight two?"

Gav nodded slowly. "Three years. Then the restructure."

"Mm." Tāo set the PAD down. "Someone ran their servers last night. Pulled a lot of interesting data about what's in those tanks." A pause. "It wasn't just construction materials."

Gav stared at them.

"The people who did the run," Tāo continued, "are going to need to know what to do with what they found. And the workers who built those facilities — who are still working them — they're going to need to know too."

They looked at Gav directly for the first time.

"So. Let's talk about what you saw up there."

—

The data is in your hands now.
What you do with it determines what kind of runner you are.
The corps are watching. So are others.
