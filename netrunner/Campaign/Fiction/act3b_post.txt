PALE HORSE

The Black Site's research archive took eleven minutes to breach.

You were inside for eight of them before the alarm triggered. The last three, you spent pulling everything you could reach: the full Antisenescence Project records, the PTR VI host range data, the communication logs between Director Kase and the Chairman's office. Forty-three months of Project Pale Horse, compressed onto a chip the size of a fingernail.

You made it out.

---

The data has been moving through the runner networks for six days.

Fragmentary at first — a partial file here, a corrupted log there, the way things spread when people are afraid to hold the whole picture. But it's moving. Checksums are matching. The runner networks are fractal, paranoid, brilliant. When something real comes through, they know.

The newsies won't touch it. Three outlets that started working on the story went quiet within forty-eight hours. The corps have informants, and enough credits to make almost anything disappear.

Almost.

---

Tāo went dark the morning after you sent the package. Their workshop is empty. The servo arms folded. No message, just an absence — which is its own kind of message.

Your contact has not responded in four days. The server address from the arcade memchip returns nothing now.

Jinteki's security division has circulated twenty-two new profiles in the last week. Runners. Workers from H-982. A clone from the Heinlein agricultural units who asked too many questions about a fellow worker who stopped showing up.

---

Project Pale Horse is not construction. It is not helium extraction. Director Kase knows that. The Chairman's office knows that. And now, passed in fragments between people who know how to read them, others are starting to know it too.

You got in. You got out. You have the data.

The corps are watching. The hunters are active.

But so is everyone who saw what you found.

What happens next isn't a run.
It's everything that runs make possible.
